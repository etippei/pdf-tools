How to Add Image to PDF - Complete Guide

Need to add a logo, photo, or graphic to your PDF? Inserting images into PDFs is a common need for branding, reports, and presentations. This guide shows you how to do it quickly and easily.

Why Add Images to PDF?

  • Branding - Add company logos to business documents
  • Visual support - Include photos and diagrams to explain concepts
  • Professional reports - Enhance presentations with charts and graphics
  • Personal documents - Add photos to resumes, portfolios, or invitations

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Access the PDF Image Tool

Visit the PDF image tool on PDFHub.AI. All processing happens locally in your browser - your files never leave your device.

Step 2: Upload Your PDF

Click the upload area or drag and drop your PDF file. The tool supports files up to 50MB.

Step 3: Upload Your Image

Select the image you want to add. Supported formats include:

Format Best For
PNG Logos, graphics with transparent backgrounds
JPG/JPEG Photos, general purpose images
GIF Simple graphics
WebP Modern format, smaller file size

Step 4: Position Your Image

After uploading, you can:

  • Drag and drop - Move the image to any position on the page
  • Resize - Click and drag corners to adjust size
  • Rotate - Rotate 90°, 180°, or custom angles

Step 5: Choose Page Placement

Option Description
Current Page Add image to the page you're viewing
All Pages Add the same image to every page
Page Range Add image to specific pages (e.g., 1-5)
First Page Only Common for cover pages and title pages

Step 6: Apply and Download

Click Add Image to insert your image into the PDF. Once complete, click Download to save your updated file.

Pro Tips for Best Results

  • Use high-quality images - Low resolution images look pixelated when inserted
  • Check file size - Large images increase PDF file size significantly
  • Maintain aspect ratio - Hold Shift while resizing to keep proportions
  • Test placement - Add the image to one page first before applying to all pages
  • Use transparent PNGs - Perfect for logos and overlays that don't block text

Common Use Cases

Case 1: Adding a Logo to Business Documents

Upload your company logo (PNG with transparent background) and place it in the top-right corner of every page.

Case 2: Inserting Photos into Reports

Add product photos or team pictures to relevant pages of your presentation.

Case 3: Creating a Cover Page

Add a background image to the first page for a professional title page.

Case 4: Adding Diagrams and Charts

Insert charts and graphs to support your data in reports.

Case 5: Personalizing Documents

Add photos to resumes, invites, or greeting cards.
