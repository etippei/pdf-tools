How to Sign PDF Documents - Digital Signature Guide

Gone are the days of printing, signing, scanning, and emailing documents. Digital signatures let you sign PDFs instantly from anywhere. This guide shows you how to add professional signatures to your documents.

Why Sign PDFs Digitally?

  • No printing needed - Save paper and ink
  • Instant delivery - Sign and send in seconds
  • Legally binding - Digital signatures are recognized by law
  • Professional appearance - Clean, consistent signatures every time

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Access the PDF Signature Tool

Visit the PDF signature tool on PDFHub.AI. All processing happens locally in your browser - your files never leave your device.

Step 2: Upload Your PDF

Click the upload area or drag and drop your PDF file. The tool supports files up to 50MB.

Step 3: Create Your Signature

You have three options to create your signature:

Method Description Best For
Draw Use your mouse or touchpad to draw Quick signing
Type Type your name, choose a font style Clean, professional look
Upload Upload an image of your handwritten signature Official documents

Step 4: Place Your Signature

  • Click on the document where you want to add the signature
  • Resize and reposition as needed
  • You can add multiple signatures to different pages

Step 5: Add Signature Details (Optional)

Field Description
Date Automatically adds today's date
Title Your job title or role
Company Organization name
Email Contact information

Step 6: Apply and Download

Click Sign Document to apply your signature. Once complete, click Download to save your signed PDF.

Pro Tips for Best Results

  • Use a clear signature - Draw slowly for better results
  • Test on one page - Place a sample signature first to check appearance
  • Keep it consistent - Use the same signature style across all documents
  • Add initials - Some documents require initials on every page
  • Save your signature - Save a digital copy to reuse later

Types of Signatures Explained

Type Description Legal Status
Simple Digital Signature Typed name or drawn signature Valid for most documents
Electronic Signature (eSignature) Encrypted digital signature Legally binding in most countries
Advanced Digital Signature Verified identity with certificate Required for high-stakes documents

Frequently Asked Questions

Is signing PDFs really free? Yes! Free users get 5 signatures per day. Pro users get unlimited signing and advanced options.

Are digital signatures legally binding? Yes. Electronic signatures are legally recognized in the US (ESIGN Act), EU (eIDAS), and most countries.

Can I sign PDFs on my phone? Yes. PDFHub.AI works on any device with a browser.

Do you store my files or signature? Never. All processing happens locally in your browser. Your signature is never stored on any server.

Can I add multiple signatures to one document? Yes. You can add signatures for multiple signers on different pages.

What file formats are supported? PDF only, with support for both digital and scanned documents.
