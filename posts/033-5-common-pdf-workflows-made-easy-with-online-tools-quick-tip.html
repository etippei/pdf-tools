5 Common PDF Workflows Made Easy with Online Tools - Quick Tips

PDFs are everywhere. But working with them often feels like a chore. Different tasks require different tools, and finding the right workflow can be confusing.

Here are 5 common PDF workflows that used to be complicated—and how to simplify them with the right online tools.

Workflow 1: Scanned Document to Editable Text

The Problem: You have a scanned contract, invoice, or form. You need to extract the text, but copying and pasting doesn't work. You're stuck typing everything manually.

The Better Way: Use OCR technology to recognize and extract text from your scanned document.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Upload your scanned PDF to the OCR Tool
  2. Click "Extract Text" for plain text, or "Export to Word" for a fully formatted DOCX
  3. Copy, edit, or save your extracted text

Why It Works: OCR (Optical Character Recognition) converts images of text into actual, editable text. No more manual typing.

Try: OCR - Extract Text from PDF

Workflow 2: Combining Multiple Documents into One

The Problem: You have several PDFs—contracts, reports, scanned pages. You need to combine them into a single document for sharing or printing.

The Better Way: Use a PDF merger to combine multiple files instantly.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Upload all your PDFs to the Merge Tool
  2. Drag and drop to reorder them as needed
  3. Click "Merge PDFs" and download your combined file

Why It Works: Merging eliminates the need to email multiple files or combine them manually.

Try: Merge PDF Files

Workflow 3: Extracting Only What You Need

The Problem: You have a 50-page report but only need pages 10-15. Sharing the whole file is unnecessary and unprofessional.

The Better Way: Extract specific pages or page ranges.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Upload your PDF to the Split Tool
  2. Choose "Extract page range" or "Extract single pages"
  3. Enter the page numbers or range (e.g., "10-15")
  4. Click "Extract Pages" and download your new PDF

Why It Works: Splitting lets you share only the relevant content—perfect for clients or colleagues.

Try: Split PDF Pages

Workflow 4: Sharing Large Files via Email

The Problem: You need to email a PDF, but it's over Gmail's 25MB limit. The email bounces back or takes forever to send.

The Better Way: Compress the PDF file size before attaching it to your email.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Upload your PDF to the Compress Tool
  2. Choose a compression level:
    • Low - Maximum compression (30-40% of original) - Best for email
    • Medium - Balanced compression (50-60% of original) - Recommended
    • High - Minimum compression (70-80% of original) - Best quality
  3. Click "Compress PDF" and download the smaller version

Why It Works: Compression reduces file size while preserving quality. Your email attachment becomes manageable.

Try: Compress PDF Files

Workflow 5: Understanding Long Documents Quickly

The Problem: You have a lengthy report, research paper, or contract. Reading the entire document takes hours, but you need the key information fast.

The Better Way: Use AI to generate summaries or ask questions about the content.

Step-by-Step for Summaries:

  1. Upload your PDF to the AI Summary Tool
  2. Choose Short, Medium, or Long summary length
  3. Get a concise, intelligent summary in seconds

Step-by-Step for Q&A:

  1. Upload your PDF to the AI Chat Tool
  2. Ask questions like "What are the key findings?" or "Summarize section 3"
  3. Get instant AI-powered answers based on your document

Why It Works: AI can quickly identify and extract the most important information from any document.

Try: AI Summary | AI Chat with PDF

Bonus Workflow: Fixing Document Orientation

The Problem: Your scanned document has pages that are upside-down or sideways.

The Better Way: Rotate specific pages or the entire document.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Upload your PDF to the Rotate Tool
  2. Choose to rotate all pages or specific pages by number (e.g., "1,3,5-7")
  3. Select the angle: 90°, 180°, or 270°
  4. Click "Rotate PDF" and download your fixed document

Try: Rotate PDF Pages
