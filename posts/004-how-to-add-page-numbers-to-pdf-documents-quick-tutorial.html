How to Add Page Numbers to PDF Documents - Quick Tutorial

Page numbers make long PDF documents easier to navigate. Whether you're preparing a business report, formatting an ebook, or organizing a legal document, adding page numbers is essential for professionalism.

Why Add Page Numbers to PDF?

  • Easy reference - Quickly find specific sections during meetings or reviews
  • Professional appearance - Polished documents impress clients and colleagues
  • Print ready - Physical copies stay organized with numbered pages
  • Table of contents - Page numbers make TOCs actually useful

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 2: Upload Your PDF

Click the upload area or drag and drop your PDF file. The tool supports files up to 50MB and documents with up to 200 pages.

Step 3: Choose Numbering Position

Select where you want page numbers to appear:

Position Best For

Step 4: Customize Numbering Options

  • Start page - Choose which page to start numbering (skip cover pages)
  • Starting number - Begin at 1, i, or any custom number
  • Format - Choose from 1,2,3 or i,ii,iii or 001,002,003

Step 5: Apply and Download

Click Add Page Numbers to process your document. Once complete, click Download to save your numbered PDF.

Pro Tips for Best Results

  • Skip cover pages - Start numbering on page 2 or 3 for professional reports
  • Use Roman numerals - Perfect for prefaces, introductions, and appendices
  • Preview first - Check one page before processing the entire document
  • Keep it simple - Bottom center is the most universally accepted position
