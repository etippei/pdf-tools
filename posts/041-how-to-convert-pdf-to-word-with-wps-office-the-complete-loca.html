How to Convert PDF to Word with WPS Office: The Complete Local Conversion Guide

You have a PDF that needs editing. You could use an online converter, but it contains sensitive information—contracts, financial data, or personal documents. Uploading it to a third-party server is risky.

WPS Office offers a better way: local PDF to Word conversion that keeps your files on your device. This guide covers everything you need to know.

Step-by-Step: Convert PDF to Word in WPS Office

Method 1: Standard Conversion

This method works for text-based PDFs (digital PDFs where text is already selectable).

Step 1: Open your PDF in WPS Office

Launch WPS Office and open the PDF file you want to convert. You can do this by:

  • Clicking File > Open and selecting your PDF
  • Dragging and dropping the PDF into the WPS window
  • Right-clicking the PDF file and selecting Open with WPS Office

Step 2: Access the conversion tool

Click Convert in the top toolbar (or File > Export PDF as > Word depending on your version)[citation:4][citation:14].

Step 3: Choose your settings

WPS offers three conversion modes[citation:7]:

Mode Description Best For
Preserve layout Maintains original formatting, spacing, and positioning Complex documents with tables and images
Preserve content only Keeps text and basic formatting but may change layout Text-heavy documents where layout isn't critical
Textbox mode Places content into text boxes for exact positioning Documents with complex layouts or overlays

Step 4: Select page range (optional)

By default, WPS converts all pages. You can specify a range (e.g., pages 1-5) if you only need part of the document.

Step 5: Click Start Conversion

Wait for WPS to process. Conversion time depends on file size and complexity—typically 30 seconds to a few minutes[citation:4].

Step 6: Save your Word document

Once conversion completes, save the file as a .docx document.

Method 2: Converting Scanned PDFs (OCR Required)

If your PDF is a scan or image-based, standard conversion won't work—the text isn't actually text. Scanned PDFs are just images of text[citation:2].

Step 1: Open your scanned PDF in WPS

Step 2: Access the OCR-enabled conversion

The conversion flow is similar, but WPS will detect that the PDF is image-based and prompt you to use OCR (Optical Character Recognition). WPS's OCR engine runs locally on your device, so files stay private[citation:2].

Step 3: Confirm OCR settings

  • Select the document language (for best accuracy)
  • Choose whether to preserve original layout
  • Click Start Conversion

Step 4: Review and save

OCR converts the images to editable text. The result is a fully editable Word document with the text extracted from your scans.

Pro tip: For best OCR results, scan documents at 300 DPI minimum and ensure pages are straight[citation:7].

Method 3: Batch Conversion

Need to convert multiple PDFs at once? WPS supports batch processing[citation:4].

Step 1: Open WPS and navigate to the PDF conversion tool Step 2: Select Batch Conversion mode Step 3: Drag and drop all your PDF files into the interface Step 4: Choose conversion settings (apply to all files) Step 5: Click Start Conversion and wait for processing

Conversion Quality: What to Expect

WPS uses a high-precision layout analysis engine to preserve document structure[citation:7].

What converts well:

Tables Good Borders, merged cells, and colors preserved

What may need cleanup:

Quick tip: If formatting looks off after conversion, try a different conversion mode. "Preserve layout" mode usually gives the best results for complex documents[citation:4].

Pro version:

For occasional use (short documents, low frequency), the free version is sufficient. For daily use or long documents, Pro is recommended[citation:2][citation:7].

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Issue: Conversion fails or produces errors

Possible causes and fixes:

Issue Solution
Password-protected PDF Remove password first (if you have permission)
File is corrupted Try opening in another reader and resave as PDF
File too large Split into smaller parts before converting
WPS version is outdated Update to the latest version[citation:4]

Issue: Formatting looks wrong

Solution: Try a different conversion mode. "Preserve layout" mode is usually best for complex documents[citation:2].

Issue: OCR doesn't work

Solution: Ensure your WPS version includes OCR (not all versions do). For older versions, you may need a dedicated OCR tool first[citation:9].

Issue: Conversion is very slow

Solution: Close other applications to free up system resources. Large files with many images take longer to process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is WPS PDF to Word conversion really free?

Basic conversion is available in the free version, but features like unlimited conversions or advanced OCR may require a subscription[citation:9].

Does WPS convert scanned PDFs?

Yes, if your WPS version includes OCR. The OCR engine runs locally, keeping your files private[citation:2].

Can I convert password-protected PDFs?

Only if you have the password. Remove the password first, then convert.

Which conversion mode should I choose?

Use Preserve layout for documents with complex formatting. Use Preserve content for text-heavy documents where layout isn't critical[citation:7].

Will my formatting stay exactly the same?

WPS aims to preserve formatting, but complex layouts may need minor adjustments. Review the document after conversion.
